financial dashboard

redesigning the core analytics experience for a b2b fintech platform — from a fragmented seven-team product into a unified, research-led system.

01 — overview

a platform that grew faster than its design

the platform processes over £2m in daily transactions across seven internal teams. each team had shipped its own flows independently — there was no shared design language, no unified navigation model, and a 40-step onboarding that lost 60% of new users before they ever reached a dashboard.

i was brought in to lead the end-to-end redesign: from initial discovery through to developer handoff, with a focus on information architecture and the creation of a scalable design system.

hero / overview image

02 — problem

seven teams, zero shared language

the core issue wasn't any single flow — it was the absence of a consistent mental model. users couldn't predict where features lived, terminology differed by team, and the dashboard surfaced data without context or hierarchy.

onboarding was the most acute symptom: 40 steps, no progress indicators, and no clear "aha moment" before users hit a paywall. churn was almost entirely front-loaded.

03 — research

getting close to the problem

i ran 18 moderated usability sessions with existing users across three tiers — power users, casual users, and new sign-ups. sessions were recorded and synthesised using an affinity map spanning 230+ observations.

i also conducted a competitive audit of six fintech platforms and an internal stakeholder alignment workshop to surface conflicting product assumptions across the seven teams.

research artefacts — affinity map / workshop output

04 — insights

three things that changed the direction

the research collapsed into three themes that reframed the brief. users weren't confused by complexity — they were confused by unpredictability. they wanted control over their view, not simplification.

05 — design

from wireframes to a design system

i started with low-fidelity wireframes to stress-test the new information architecture before any visual decisions were made. three rounds of prototype testing shaped the navigation model and widget hierarchy.

from there i built a component library in figma — 80+ components across 6 token categories — and ran a naming workshop with engineering to ensure the system could be implemented without translation overhead.

06 — solution

a unified analytics experience

the final dashboard introduced a persistent navigation rail, a unified token system, and a customisable widget grid that let users build their own view without losing the default experience. onboarding was reduced to 8 steps with deferred configuration.
